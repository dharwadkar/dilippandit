A Diabolical Cross Connection

In an age when a ubiquitous cell phone had not become a reality and when landline phones were used frequently, cross-connections did take place on rare occasions. This happened when one call is rudely interrupted by another call and a new voice seeks your urgent attention. I experienced one such cross- connection sometime during late nineties when I worked in the the erstwhile Delhi Vidyut Board as its Secretary. This call led to quite bizarre and unexpected development as the following true story will illustrate.

"Hello what did you say? Wait I think there is cross connection. Someone else is trying to speak to me. Could you please hang up for a minute. Let me see who is at the other end." I told my friend to whom I was talking when suddenly out of the blue another voice interrupted my existing call and insisted on talking to me.

My friend hung up as advised. And then the person at the other end came clearly on the line.

“Who are you?” He asked me.
"Who do you wish to speak with?"" I asked in return. I get quite irritated with people who ring up and ask you your name without confirming the number they have dialled.
"I am Surendra Gupta from Chandni Chowk" he replied and enquired whether his friend one Mr. Rohit Sharma was on the line
"I think you should check the number you have dialled". I told him as I prepared to put down the receiver.
"Just wait. Who are you anyway?"" He insisted on asking my name.
"Well if you insist I am Dilip Pandit".

This is how our initial conversation started. He asked me what I do and told me what he does. It seems that he was in some kind of printing business in Chandni Chowk. When he came to know that I was working for Delhi Vidyut Board as Secretary he tried to be a little friendly. He also told me that he is an Astrologer and makes birth chart of people as a side business. He offered to make one for me and my children if I could give him their date, place and time of birth.

I was not too keen on taking this conversation forward with someone whom I had not yet personally met. However I thought there should be no problem in sharing details of birth date with someone just to see if he is serious about what he says he does. I was stupid. He had a way of making people believe him. A perfect con man.

Sure enough he sent a huge packet containing print outs of birth chart and details of all astrological charts of my family sans my wife a few weeks later. He appeared to be genuine so far.

Then started what I, in retrospect, thought was an elaborate attempt at influencing me in playing his diabolical game. He told me that if I do as he bid me to do I shall gain in power and money. I do not know what happened to me. I started believing what he said on phone without yet meeting him. If I were to describe what he tried to do with me now, twenty years later, it will surely be called as trying to hypnotise a potential victim. The matters came to a head when his call came about a month after we spoke for the first time thanks to a cross-connection on an MTNL (Mahanagar Telecom Nigam Limited) line.

"Mr Pandit, I tell you to go to Rama Vihar where you will find a half finished building that I am constructing. I will give you exact address when you decide to go. When you enter the building take a right turn and enter a room and urinate on the southern wall of the room." If that sounds like an idiotic and stupid way to describe this conversation then I don’t blame you. But this is what precisely he said.

"Look I don’t have time to do this. And frankly I don’t think we should take this forward anymore" I tried to tell him as calmly as possible.

"But you don’t understand Pandit sahab. I promise you would not regret doing this. Many of my friends have benefitted enormously by doing this." He sounded slightly irritated at having been told where to get off.

"I will let you know what I intend to do in a few days’ time." I said just to buy time and and get rid of him on phone without sounding impolite.

My wife was near me overhearing our conversation. She was quite perplexed at the long rope I seem to be giving him. I too felt that this fellow, who I have never met is trying to cast a spell on me. Yet I felt helpless.

When his phone came the following day to ask what I intended to do I had made up my mind.

"Look Mr. Gupta, I am fed up of your calls and I am not doing anything that you have suggested to me. Either you stop bothering me or I shall report the matter to the Police." I said sternly and at the same time feeling relieved at the finality of my decision that I conveyed to him in no uncertain terms.

After that he never rang up.

But a few weeks later I read in the paper that his printing press has been raided by the anti-theft squad of Delhi Vidyut Board for theft and that his printing press been slapped a bill of Rs. 10 lacs. When I checked up with my staff it appeared that he was using an unauthorised 3-phase 11A KW connection without meter for quite sometime. A rascal indeed.

This was how the story of the initial diabolical cross connection ended.
